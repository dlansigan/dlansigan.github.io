About

I am a 4th year undergraduate Mechanical Engineering student at the University of California, Berkeley. I hope to enter a career in aerospace, preferrably in research and development. In order to reach this goal, I aim to earn a Ph. D. in Mechanical Engineering studying fluids.

Specifically, my research interests are in computational fluid dynamics (CFD) and machine learning. I am currently an undergraduate researcher in Dr. Philip Marcus's CFD Lab, where I am working on aerodynamic shape optimization using deep learning. I hope to continue similar research in graduate school.

I have also had experience in industry. The summer of my sophomore year, I interned at Microsoft as an Explorer Intern, which is a program management and software development rotation internship. The summer of my senior year, I interned at The Aerospace Corporation as a Computer Aided Engineering (CAE) Intern. As part of the CAE team, I designed and built 3D printed models and developed software tools for other engineers in the company. More details about my internship experiences are in my CV.
