Midterms

He was a Vietnam vet
and he was teaching
our literature class for the day.
He snatched the

school paper from
the whiteboard
and opened it up
to the center spread.

He held it up
and pointed to COLLEGE MAJORS,
bolded, and told us,
"When you choose

what you want to do
for the
rest of your life,
you only need to

consider one thing."
He looked at it for
a while, bloodshot
eyes scanning

The colored boxes,
and jabbed two fingers
at the orange one
labeled ENGINEERING.

"Pick the hardest one
because there's a reason
it's hard and it's 'cause
it's what pays most."

So here I am now,
studying for a midterm
I don't want to take
but will help me

do what I want.
