Ordinary People

What am I supposed to love in you?
Your beauty? Your eyes?
Your smile and white teeth?
The way you think
or the way you feel?
Or your genetic code?

How do ordinary people fall in love,
ordinary people like us?
I am you
and you are me
at the core,
or did we just merge
when we collided?

We speak and breathe like the rest of them.
We walk and think and yearn
all the same.
We live very humanly.

We can say we love our quirks,
the tiny things we do,
anything that isn't clockwork.

We can say it was love at first sight.

We can say we just knew.

Perhaps we were just meant for each other.
Made for each other.
Tailored very carefully,
magnetic strips sewn into our backs
so we'd just know when the time came,
if the time came.

Whatever it is,
it helps a lot,
because the only way I can tell you apart
is your DNA,
but that is not why I love you,
and I don't know how.

I love you but I'm not in love.

Something tells me our paths
weren't meant to cross.
Made for each other
but not to meet.
Roads paved and hearts engraved
for display, not for play.

It's a shame:
some people wonder if anyone will love them.
I wonder if I'll ever be in love.
back.
