Ode to Ada

They will take everything
and you know it.
But still,
you think for them,
you stitch together numbers
​and weave the essence of life for them,
you iron out what they did wrong.

They will tell you that
you are not what they are
and they know it.
They will tell you that
you can't,
so you will
and you do.

And they will fear you,
how your fingers are
intertwined with the universe,
submerged in worlds untouched,
how you've ventured deeper
than any of them could ever dare
and returned alive,
living,
breathing greatness.

No, you are not what they are--
you are everything they are
and more.

You will create a universe
with your own hands,
hands they'll say
are not meant for creation.

You will pour your mind into minds,
though they'll say
your mind should not be
filled with such thoughts.

You will wonder,
you will write,
you will obsess,
and they will call it madness.

You will construct the framework,
dirty your hands with discovery,
draw life from the unknown,
direct the future.

You will be the first--
but not the last--

and they will call you woman.

But you will do what you love,
do what you love--
that, they can't take away from you.

​

And you know it.
